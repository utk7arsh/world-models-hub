# Are our benchmarks measuring general world-modeling ability or narrow overfitting?

[[revisiting-ale]] showed that Atari scores were quietly gamed by exploiting determinism, and [[procgen]] exposed huge train-test generalization gaps once levels were procedurally varied. Broad-competence suites like [[crafter]] and diagnostic ones like [[bsuite]] were built precisely because single-number leaderboards mislead, a worry captured in [[benchmarks-shape-progress]]. As world models saturate Atari 100k, are we measuring real understanding or overfitting to a handful of games and protocols? Choosing the next benchmark may matter more than the next architecture.
