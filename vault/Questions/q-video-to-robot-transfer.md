# Can world models learned from internet video transfer to controlling real robots?

[[v-jepa2]] and [[unisim]] claim that models pretrained on large, mostly passive video can be turned into planners that drive real hardware zero-shot, and platforms like [[cosmos]] and navigation models like [[nwm]] extend the promise. But passive video lacks actions, contact forces, and the consequences of intervention, and [[daydreamer]] showed some embodied skills still demand real on-robot experience. How much genuine control transfers from watching versus doing, and where does the reality gap reopen? This is the central bet of physical-AI world models.
