# Predictive Coding in the Visual Cortex: A Functional Interpretation of Some Extra-classical Receptive-field Effects

> The influential neuroscience model proposing the cortex is a hierarchical generative predictor that only transmits prediction errors upward.

Introduces a hierarchical model in which higher visual areas predict the activity of lower areas via feedback connections, while feedforward connections carry only the residual prediction error. Fitting this scheme to natural images reproduces non-classical receptive-field phenomena such as end-stopping. It established predictive coding as a leading account of perception as inference over an internal generative model of the world.
