# Formal Theory of Creativity, Fun, and Intrinsic Motivation (1990-2010)

> A unifying theory casting curiosity and creativity as intrinsic reward for improving a predictive/compressive world model.

Formalizes intrinsic motivation as reward proportional to the agent's rate of progress in compressing (predicting) its sensory history. An agent is driven to seek data its world model can still learn from but has not yet mastered, naturally producing exploration, play, and 'interestingness' while avoiding both fully predictable and fully random stimuli. It provides the theoretical grounding for curiosity-driven world-model learning.
