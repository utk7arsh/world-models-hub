# Neuroscience frames the cortex as a hierarchical generative model that transmits only prediction errors.

World-model research carries a deep debt to theoretical neuroscience. [[rao-ballard-pc]] proposed that the visual cortex is a hierarchy of generative predictors in which feedback connections carry predictions downward and feedforward connections carry only the residual prediction error upward — a scheme that reproduces puzzling receptive-field effects. [[friston-fep]] generalized this into the free-energy principle, casting perception, learning, and action as one process of minimizing the surprise of sensory inputs against an internal model. These ideas gave biological legitimacy to the engineering claim, argued in [[lecun-path-ami]], that intelligence is organized around a predictive world model. They also motivate the representational-prediction stance captured in [[predict-features-not-pixels]]: brains do not reconstruct their retinas, they infer compact causes, which is exactly what feature-predicting architectures try to imitate.
