# Engram — A Second Brain for World Models

Welcome to Engram, a living map of research on world models — learned internal simulators that let machines predict, imagine, and plan. The map is organized into eight lobes. It starts with [[foundations-theory]], where [[dyna]] framed planning as learning from imagined data and neuroscience cast the brain as a predictor. From there the [[latent-imagination]] lobe traces [[world-models]] into the Dreamer family up to [[dreamerv3]]; [[planning-mcts]] follows [[muzero]] and the value-equivalence idea; and [[autoregressive-wm]] tokenizes the world for transformers like [[iris]]. The frontier lobes push further: [[video-diffusion-wm]] with playable simulators like [[genie]], [[jepa]] built on [[lecun-path-ami]] and [[i-jepa]], and [[embodied-agents]] where models leave the lab for real robots. [[benchmarks-eval]] keeps everyone honest. If you read nothing else, start with the hub notes that stitch the graph together: [[learning-in-imagination]], [[value-equivalence-principle]], [[predict-features-not-pixels]], and [[video-as-world-simulator]]. Each paper links to its ancestors and descendants, and the concept notes link generously across lobes. Follow the wikilinks in any direction; the graph rewards wandering, and every path eventually loops back to the same central bet — that a good enough model of the world is the foundation of intelligence.
